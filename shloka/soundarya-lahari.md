# Soundarya Lahari

By Adi Shankaracharya

Soundarya Lahari ("The Wave of Beauty") is one of the most celebrated hymns in Sanskrit literature. Composed of 100 verses, it praises the beauty, grace, and power of Goddess Shakti (Parvati). The first 41 verses (Ananda Lahari — "Wave of Bliss") describe the mystical and tantric aspects of the Goddess, while verses 42–100 (Soundarya Lahari proper) describe her physical beauty from head to toe.

## Verse 1

```
शिवः शक्त्या युक्तो यदि भवति शक्तः प्रभवितुं
न चेदेवं देवो न खलु कुशलः स्पन्दितुमपि ।
अतस्त्वामाराध्यां हरिहरविरिञ्च्यादिभिरपि
प्रणन्तुं स्तोतुं वा कथमकृतपुण्यः प्रभवति ॥
```

<details><summary><b>शिवः शक्त्या युक्तो यदि भवति शक्तः प्रभवितुं</b> — Shiva, united with Shakti, becomes able to create</summary>

शिवः (śivaḥ): Shiva, the auspicious one

शक्त्या (śaktyā): with Shakti, with power (instrumental of śakti)

युक्तः (yuktaḥ): united, joined

यदि (yadi): if

भवति (bhavati): becomes

शक्तः (śaktaḥ): able, capable

प्रभवितुम् (prabhavitum): to create, to manifest (infinitive of pra + bhū)

</details>

<details><summary><b>न चेदेवं देवो न खलु कुशलः स्पन्दितुमपि</b> — If not so, the God is not even able to stir</summary>

न (na): not

चेत् (cet): if

एवम् (evam): thus, in this way

देवः (devaḥ): God, the shining one

न (na): not

खलु (khalu): indeed, certainly

कुशलः (kuśalaḥ): capable, skilled

स्पन्दितुम् (spanditum): to vibrate, to stir, to move even slightly (infinitive of spand)

अपि (api): even

</details>

<details><summary><b>अतस्त्वामाराध्यां हरिहरविरिञ्च्यादिभिरपि</b> — Therefore You, who are worshipped even by Hari, Hara, and Virinchi (Brahma)</summary>

अतः (ataḥ): therefore, hence

त्वाम् (tvām): You (accusative)

आराध्याम् (ārādhyām): worthy of worship, the one who is worshipped

हरि (hari): Vishnu

हर (hara): Shiva

विरिञ्चि (viriñci): Brahma

आदिभिः (ādibhiḥ): and others (instrumental plural)

अपि (api): even

</details>

<details><summary><b>प्रणन्तुं स्तोतुं वा कथमकृतपुण्यः प्रभवति</b> — How can one who has not earned merit be able to bow to or praise You?</summary>

प्रणन्तुम् (praṇantum): to bow, to salute (infinitive of pra + nam)

स्तोतुम् (stotum): to praise, to hymn (infinitive of stu)

वा (vā): or

कथम् (katham): how

अकृतपुण्यः (akṛtapuṇyaḥ): one who has not earned merit (a = not, kṛta = done, puṇya = merit)

प्रभवति (prabhavati): is able, is capable

</details>

## Verse 2

```
तनीयांसं पांसुं तव चरणपङ्केरुहभवं
विरिञ्चिः सञ्चिन्वन् विरचयति लोकानविकलम् ।
वहत्येनं शौरिः कथमपि सहस्रेण शिरसां
हरः सङ्क्षुद्यैनं भजति भसितोद्धूलनविधिम् ॥

तनीयांसम् (tanīyāṃsam): the finest, most subtle (comparative of tanu = thin)

पांसुम् (pāṃsum): dust, particle

तव (tava): Your

चरण (caraṇa): foot

पङ्केरुह (paṅkeruha): lotus (paṅka = mud, eruha = growing from — "that which grows from mud")

भवम् (bhavam): born from, arising from

</details>

<details><summary><b>विरिञ्चिः सञ्चिन्वन् विरचयति लोकानविकलम्</b> — Brahma, gathering it, creates the worlds without flaw</summary>

विरिञ्चिः (viriñciḥ): Brahma, the creator

सञ्चिन्वन् (sañcinvan): gathering, collecting (present participle of sam + ci)

विरचयति (viracayati): creates, fashions (causative of vi + rac)

लोकान् (lokān): worlds (accusative plural)

अविकलम् (avikalam): without flaw, perfectly (a = not, vikala = impaired)

</details>

<details><summary><b>वहत्येनं शौरिः कथमपि सहस्रेण शिरसां</b> — Vishnu bears this dust somehow upon his thousand heads</summary>

वहति (vahati): bears, carries

एनम् (enam): this (accusative, referring to the dust)

शौरिः (śauriḥ): Vishnu (descendant of Śūra, i.e., Krishna/Vishnu)

कथमपि (kathamapi): somehow, with difficulty

सहस्रेण (sahasreṇa): with a thousand (instrumental)

शिरसाम् (śirasām): of heads (genitive plural of śiras)

</details>

<details><summary><b>हरः सङ्क्षुद्यैनं भजति भसितोद्धूलनविधिम्</b> — Shiva, crushing it, performs the rite of smearing sacred ash</summary>

हरः (haraḥ): Shiva, the destroyer

सङ्क्षुद्य (saṅkṣudya): crushing, grinding (gerund of sam + kṣud)

एनम् (enam): this

भजति (bhajati): practices, performs, resorts to

भसित (bhasita): sacred ash

उद्धूलन (uddhūlana): smearing, dusting upon the body

विधिम् (vidhim): rite, practice, method

</details>

</details>

<details><summary><b>जडानां चैतन्यस्तबकमकरन्दश्रुतिझरी</b> — For the dull-witted, You are the flowing stream of nectar from the bouquet of consciousness</summary>

भजन्ति त्वां धन्याः कतिचन चिदानन्दलहरीम् ॥
```

<details><summary><b>सुधासिन्धोर्मध्ये सुरविटपिवाटीपरिवृते</b> — In the midst of the ocean of nectar, surrounded by groves of celestial wish-fulfilling trees</summary>

सुधा (sudhā): nectar, ambrosia

सिन्धोः (sindhoḥ): of the ocean (genitive of sindhu)

मध्ये (madhye): in the middle

सुर (sura): divine, celestial

विटपि (viṭapi): tree (specifically wish-fulfilling trees)

वाटी (vāṭī): garden, grove

परिवृते (parivṛte): surrounded (locative, past participle of pari + vṛ)

</details>

<details><summary><b>मणिद्वीपे नीपोपवनवति चिन्तामणिगृहे</b> — On the Island of Gems, with gardens of Kadamba trees, in the house of wish-fulfilling jewels</summary>

मणि (maṇi): gem, jewel

द्वीपे (dvīpe): on the island (locative of dvīpa)

नीप (nīpa): the Kadamba tree (Nauclea cadamba)

उपवनवति (upavanavati): having gardens (upavana = garden, -vat = possessing)

चिन्तामणि (cintāmaṇi): wish-fulfilling gem

गृहे (gṛhe): in the house (locative of gṛha)

</details>

<details><summary><b>शिवाकारे मञ्चे परमशिवपर्यङ्कनिलयां</b> — On a couch in the form of Shiva, reclining upon Paramashiva as the bed</summary>

शिव (śiva): Shiva

आकारे (ākāre): in the form of (locative of ākāra)

मञ्चे (mañce): on the couch, on the platform (locative of mañca)

परमशिव (paramaśiva): the Supreme Shiva

पर्यङ्क (paryaṅka): bed, couch

निलयाम् (nilayām): resting, dwelling (accusative feminine)

</details>

<details><summary><b>भजन्ति त्वां धन्याः कतिचन चिदानन्दलहरीम्</b> — A few blessed ones worship You, who are the wave of consciousness-bliss</summary>

भजन्ति (bhajanti): worship, adore (3rd person plural of bhaj)

त्वाम् (tvām): You (accusative)

धन्याः (dhanyāḥ): blessed, fortunate ones

कतिचन (katicana): a few, some

चित् (cit): consciousness

आनन्द (ānanda): bliss

लहरीम् (laharīm): wave (accusative of laharī)

</details>

## Verse 9

```
महीं मूलाधारे कमपि मणिपूरे हुतवहं
स्थितं स्वाधिष्ठाने हृदि मरुतमाकाशमुपरि ।
मनोऽपि भ्रूमध्ये सकलमपि भित्त्वा कुलपथं
सहस्रारे पद्मे सह रहसि पत्या विहरसि ॥
```

<details><summary><b>महीं मूलाधारे कमपि मणिपूरे हुतवहं</b> — Earth in the Muladhara, water in the Manipura, fire</summary>

महीम् (mahīm): earth (accusative of mahī)

मूलाधारे (mūlādhāre): in the Muladhara (the root chakra; mūla = root, ādhāra = support)

कम् अपि (kam api): water (ka = water, api — here kam refers to the water element)

मणिपूरे (maṇipūre): in the Manipura (the navel chakra; maṇi = jewel, pūra = city)

हुतवहम् (hutavaham): fire (literally "carrier of oblations"; huta = offered, vaha = carrier)

</details>

<details><summary><b>स्थितं स्वाधिष्ठाने हृदि मरुतमाकाशमुपरि</b> — Stationed in the Svadhisthana, air in the heart, space above</summary>

स्थितम् (sthitam): stationed, placed

स्वाधिष्ठाने (svādhiṣṭhāne): in the Svadhisthana (the sacral chakra; sva = self, adhiṣṭhāna = seat)

हृदि (hṛdi): in the heart (locative of hṛd — referring to the Anahata chakra)

मरुतम् (marutam): air, wind (accusative)

आकाशम् (ākāśam): space, ether

उपरि (upari): above (referring to the Vishuddha/throat chakra)

</details>

<details><summary><b>मनोऽपि भ्रूमध्ये सकलमपि भित्त्वा कुलपथं</b> — Mind too in the center of the brows — having pierced through the entire Kula path</summary>

मनः (manaḥ): mind

अपि (api): also, too

भ्रूमध्ये (bhrūmadhye): in the center of the brows (bhrū = brow, madhya = middle — the Ajna chakra)

सकलम् (sakalam): entire, all

अपि (api): also

भित्त्वा (bhittvā): having pierced, having broken through (gerund of bhid)

कुलपथम् (kulapatham): the Kula path (the path of Kundalini through the chakras)

</details>

<details><summary><b>सहस्रारे पद्मे सह रहसि पत्या विहरसि</b> — In the thousand-petalled lotus, You sport secretly with Your lord</summary>

सहस्रारे (sahasrāre): in the Sahasrara (the crown chakra; sahasra = thousand, āra = spokes/petals)

पद्मे (padme): in the lotus

सह (saha): with, together with

रहसि (rahasi): in secret, in privacy

पत्या (patyā): with the lord, with the husband (instrumental of pati)

विहरसि (viharasi): You sport, You play (2nd person singular of vi + hṛ)

</details>

## Verse 10

```
सुधाधारासारैः चरणयुगलान्तर्विगलितैः
प्रपञ्चं सिञ्चन्ती पुनरपि रसाम्नायमहसः ।
अवाप्य स्वां भूमिं भुजगनिभमध्युष्टवलयं
स्वमात्मानं कृत्वा स्वपिषि कुलकुण्डे कुहरिणि ॥
```

<details><summary><b>सुधाधारासारैः चरणयुगलान्तर्विगलितैः</b> — With torrents of nectar streaming from between Your two feet</summary>

सुधा (sudhā): nectar

धारा (dhārā): stream, flow

सारैः (sāraiḥ): with torrents, with showers (instrumental plural of sāra)

चरण (caraṇa): foot

युगल (yugala): pair, couple

अन्तः (antaḥ): between, within

विगलितैः (vigalitaiḥ): flowing down, streaming (instrumental plural of vigalita)

</details>

<details><summary><b>प्रपञ्चं सिञ्चन्ती पुनरपि रसाम्नायमहसः</b> — Drenching the universe, and again from the splendor of the nectar-tradition</summary>

प्रपञ्चम् (prapañcam): the manifest universe

सिञ्चन्ती (siñcantī): drenching, sprinkling (feminine present participle of sic)

पुनः (punaḥ): again

अपि (api): also

रस (rasa): nectar, essence

आम्नाय (āmnāya): tradition, sacred text (here referring to the channels of esoteric practice)

महसः (mahasaḥ): from the splendor, from the light (ablative of mahas)

</details>

<details><summary><b>अवाप्य स्वां भूमिं भुजगनिभमध्युष्टवलयं</b> — Having reached Your own abode, coiled like a serpent in three and a half coils</summary>

अवाप्य (avāpya): having reached, having obtained (gerund of ava + āp)

स्वाम् (svām): own

भूमिम् (bhūmim): place, abode

भुजग (bhujaga): serpent

निभम् (nibham): resembling, like

अध्युष्ट (adhyuṣṭa): three and a half (referring to the coils of Kundalini)

वलयम् (valayam): coil, ring, bracelet

</details>

<details><summary><b>स्वमात्मानं कृत्वा स्वपिषि कुलकुण्डे कुहरिणि</b> — Making Yourself into Your own self, You sleep in the Kula-kunda, the hollow cavity</summary>

स्वम् (svam): own

आत्मानम् (ātmānam): self

कृत्वा (kṛtvā): having made (gerund of kṛ)

स्वपिषि (svapiṣi): You sleep (2nd person singular of svap)

कुलकुण्डे (kulakuṇḍe): in the Kula-kunda (the pit/reservoir at the base; kula = family/lineage, kuṇḍa = pit/pool)

कुहरिणि (kuhariṇi): in the hollow, in the cavity (locative of kuhariṇī)

</details>

## Key Recurring Words

शिव (śiva): the auspicious one, Lord Shiva — represents pure consciousness

शक्ति (śakti): power, energy — the dynamic creative force of the Divine Feminine

चरण (caraṇa): foot — symbolizing the grace of the Goddess

पद्म (padma): lotus — symbol of purity and spiritual unfoldment

लहरी (laharī): wave — representing the flowing nature of beauty and bliss

चिन्तामणि (cintāmaṇi): wish-fulfilling gem — the Goddess grants all desires

कुल (kula): family, lineage — refers to the Kundalini/Shakta path

सुधा (sudhā): nectar, ambrosia — the divine bliss that flows from spiritual realization

अनङ्ग (anaṅga): bodiless, Kamadeva — the god of love, empowered by Devi's grace
